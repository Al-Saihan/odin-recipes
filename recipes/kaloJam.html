How to make delicious Kalo Jam!

Description:

This traditional Bengali sweet is prepared using flour dumplings that are deep-fried until dark brown in color and then soaked in slow-simmering sugar syrup.

Ingredients:

  • Powdered Milk: 1 Cup
  • All Purpose Flour: 2 Tablespoons
  • Semolina: 1 Tablespoon
  • Baking Powder: 1 Teaspoon
  • Ghee [Clarified Butter]: 1/2 Tablespoon
  • Milk: 1/2 Cup
  • Red Food Color: 2-3 Drops
  • Water: 3 Cups
  • Sugar: 2 Cups
  • Green Cardamom: 3 pieces
  • Vegetable Virgin Oil: As Needed

Cooking Steps:

  1. Take a large round bowl, and add Powdered Milk, All Purpose Flour, Semolina, Baking Powder, and mix well.
  2. After the initial mixing is done, add the Ghee and mix well again.
  3. Slowly add the Milk, one tablespoon at a time, and mix well each time. Wait a few seconds before adding the next spoon of milk while continuously mixing the whole time.
  4. Add the Food Color, and continue mixing until the mixture becomes really sticky.
  5. Use some Ghee on your hands, spreading it nicely. Then carefully take a small portion of the mixture and shape it.
  6. First, roll the portion into a ball using your hands, and then slowly press the palms of your hands together, so that the portion turns into a sort of cylinder shape.
  7. In a separate large pan, heat the Water, Sugar, and Cardamoms, stirring with a long teaspoon, until the sugar dissolves. Keep this sugar mixture hot.
  8. In a non-stick frying pan, use Vegetable Virgin Oil to coat the surface, and with a bit more oil, fry the cylinder-shaped sweet mixtures on low-medium heat.
  9. Use a wooden spoon to slowly roll the mixtures so they cook evenly.
  10. Once the sweets turn reddish-black, soak them in the sugar mixture you made earlier and leave them in it for at least 6 hours!
  11. Finally, after waiting long enough, eat and enjoy!

Final Words:
